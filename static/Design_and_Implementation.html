"Dark mode" - To improve accessibility for low vision readers and people with light sensitivity, the background is black with white text, rather than white with black text.

Implementation

All pages were created using HTML in TextEdit on a MacBook Pro.

All pages have a black background with white text. Buttons/Boxes are gray.

All unclicked links are gold. Clicked links are pink. When hovering, links turn red and underlined.

All images have detailed alt text descriptions.

All text is enlarged (to at least 20pt).

All buttons are enlarged and have a color distinctly different than both text and the background.

Button navigation is inspired by and modeled after that of johnleeclark.com.

Consistent button placement: each subpage has a "Back" button at the top to return to the main page.

HTML tags are used consistently and with screen readers in mind.

Resources listed convey information across several sensory input modalities. Examples include but are not limited to videos (video and audio) and articles (print and sometimes audio). [Audiobooks?]
